### Activity 2: Magic Square

Fill in the missing numbers so that the sum of the numbers in each row, each column, and both diagonals is the same.

```
 8 |  1  |  6
---|---- |---
 3 |  5  |  ?  (The missing number is 7 to make the sum 15 in each row and column)
---|---- |---
 4 |  ?  |  2  (The missing number is 9 to make the sum 15 in each row and column)
```

**Answer:**
```
 8 |  1  |  6
---|---- |---
 3 |  5  |  7
---|---- |---
 4 |  9  |  2
```

### Activity 5: Fraction Fun

There are some shaded and unshaded parts in the figures below. Write the fraction that represents the shaded part.

Figure A: A circle divided into 4 equal parts with 3 shaded.
Figure B: A rectangle divided into 6 equal parts with 2 shaded.

**Answers:**
Figure A: 3/4 or Three-fourths are shaded.
Figure B: 2/6 or One-third (simplified from two-sixths) are shaded.

### Activity 6: Word Problems

Solve the following word problems and choose the correct operation.

1. Sarah had 24 apples. She gave 6 apples to her friend. How many apples does she have left?

   **Answer:** 24 - 6 = 18 apples left.

2. A baker has 9 cupcakes. If he packs them into boxes of 3, how many boxes can he fill?

   **Answer:** 9 ÷ 3 = 3 boxes.

3. John has 3 times as many marbles as Peter. If Peter has 15 marbles, how many does John have?

   **Answer:** 3 × 15 = 45 marbles.

### Activity 7: Estimation Game

Estimate the result of the following operations practically.

1. About how many pencils are there if you have 24 packs with each pack having 12 pencils?

   **Answer:** Approximately 24 × 10 = 240 pencils (a closer estimate would be 288, but we are estimating).

2. If a cake is cut into 16 slices and 2 slices are eaten, estimate how much of the cake is left.

   **Answer:** About 14/16 or roughly 7/8 of the cake is left.
