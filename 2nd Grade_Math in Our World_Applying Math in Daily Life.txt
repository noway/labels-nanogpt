# Applying Math in Daily Life

One of the most exciting things about math is that you can find it everywhere in the world around you! Every day, we use math without even realizing it. Let's explore some ways that math helps us in our daily lives.

### At the Grocery Store

When you go shopping for fruits with your parents, you can see math in action. Grocers often sell fruits by the pound. 

**Exercise 1:** If apples cost $2 per pound and you buy 3 pounds, how much would the apples cost in total?

*Step 1: Multiply the cost per pound by the number of pounds.*
**Answer:** \(2 \times 3 = 6\). The apples would cost $6 in total.

### In the Kitchen

Cooking and baking are like fun math experiments. Measuring ingredients is a practical way to use math.

**Exercise 2:** If a cookie recipe calls for 2 cups of flour and you want to make half of the recipe, how much flour do you need?

*Step 1: Divide the amount of flour by 2.*
**Answer:** \(2 \div 2 = 1\). You would need 1 cup of flour for half of the recipe.

### Planning Your Time

Imagine you have school in the morning, soccer practice in the afternoon, and homework in the evening. Understanding time helps you plan your day.

**Exercise 3:** School ends at 3 PM and soccer practice starts at 4 PM. How much time do you have between school and soccer?

*Step 1: Find the difference between the two times.*
**Answer:** \(4 - 3 = 1\). You have 1 hour between school and soccer practice.

### Sharing Equally

Say you have 12 candies and want to share them equally with 3 friends.

**Exercise 5:** How many candies does each friend get?

*Step 1: Divide the total number of candies by the number of friends.*
**Answer:** \(12 \div 3 = 4\). Each friend gets 4 candies.
