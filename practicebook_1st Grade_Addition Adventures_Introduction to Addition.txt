## What is Addition?

Addition is combining two or more numbers to find their total. When we add, the numbers we combine are called "addends," and the total is called the "sum."

For example:
- If you have 2 apples and you pick 3 more apples, you can use addition to find out how many apples you have in total.
- 2 apples + 3 apples equals 5 apples. So, 2 and 3 are the addends, and 5 is the sum.

## Symbols in Addition

In addition, we use the plus sign (+) to show that we are adding numbers together. We also use the equals sign (=) to show the result. Here is what an addition problem looks like:

2 + 3 = 5

In this equation:
- "2" and "3" are the addends.
- The "+" is the sign telling us to add.
- "5" is the sum or the total.
- The "=" sign shows that the numbers on either side of it are equal.

### Exercise 3: Addition Word Problems
Now, let's apply what we've learned to solve some word problems!

1. Sally has 3 balloons, and she buys 5 more. How many balloons does she have now?
   3 + 5 = 8 balloons

2. Mike has 2 pieces of candy, and his friend gives him 4 more. Now, how many pieces of candy does Mike have?
   2 + 4 = 6 pieces of candy

3. There are 6 ducks in a pond. 2 more ducks join them. How many ducks are in the pond now?

Remember, when solving a word problem, read carefully to find out which numbers you need to add together!
