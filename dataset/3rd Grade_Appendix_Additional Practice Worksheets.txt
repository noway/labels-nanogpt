# Additional Practice Worksheets

### Appendix

The following additional practice worksheets are designed to reinforce the key concepts and skills that 3rd grade students have learned in their mathematics coursework. These exercises will help students solidify their understanding of number operations, fractions, measurement, geometry, and data interpretation.

#### Worksheet 1: Addition and Subtraction

**Example:**
Solve the following problems
1. 345 + 278 = ?
2. 650 - 324 = ?

**Answers:**
1. 345 + 278 = 623
2. 650 - 324 = 326

**Exercises:**

1. 123 + 456 = ?
2. 789 - 321 = ?
3. 564 + 237 = ?
4. 902 - 578 = ?
5. 641 + 159 = ?

**Answers:**

1. 123 + 456 = 579
2. 789 - 321 = 468
3. 564 + 237 = 801
4. 902 - 578 = 324
5. 641 + 159 = 800

#### Worksheet 2: Multiplication and Division

**Example:**
Solve the following problems
1. 7 x 6 = ?
2. 48 ÷ 8 = ?

**Answers:**
1. 7 x 6 = 42
2. 48 ÷ 8 = 6

**Exercises:**

1. 8 x 5 = ?
2. 36 ÷ 6 = ?
3. 7 x 9 = ?
4. 63 ÷ 7 = ?
5. 4 x 8 = ?

**Answers:**

1. 8 x 5 = 40
2. 36 ÷ 6 = 6
3. 7 x 9 = 63
4. 63 ÷ 7 = 9
5. 4 x 8 = 32

#### Worksheet 3: Understanding Fractions

**Example:**
Write the fraction for the shaded part of each shape.

**Answers:**
1. 3/4 for a shape with 4 sections where 3 are shaded.
2. 1/2 for a shape with 2 sections where 1 is shaded.

**Exercises:**

Identify the shaded fraction:
1. A shape with 5 sections where 2 are shaded. Fraction = ?
2. A shape with 8 sections where 3 are shaded. Fraction = ?
3. A shape with 6 sections where 1 is shaded. Fraction = ?
4. A shape with 3 sections where 2 are shaded. Fraction = ?
5. A shape with 4 sections where all are shaded. Fraction = ?

**Answers:**

1. Fraction = 2/5
2. Fraction = 3/8
3. Fraction = 1/6
4. Fraction = 2/3
5. Fraction = 4/4 or 1

#### Worksheet 4: Measurement (Length and Time)

**Example:**
1. If a pencil is 7 inches long, how many inches are 5 pencils laid end to end?
2. If an event starts at 3:15 PM and lasts for 45 minutes, what time will it end?

**Answers:**
1. 5 pencils = 35 inches
2. The event will end at 4:00 PM

**Exercises:**

1. A book is 8 inches wide. How wide would 3 books be if placed side by side?
2. Sarah starts reading at 10:30 AM and reads for 1 hour and 15 minutes. What time will she finish reading?

**Answers:**

1. 3 books = 24 inches
2. Sarah will finish reading at 11:45 AM

#### Worksheet 5: Geometry (Shapes and Angles)

**Example:**
1. How many sides does a triangle have?
2. Identify the shape with 4 equal sides and all 90-degree angles.

**Answers:**
1. A triangle has 3 sides.
2. The shape is a square.

**Exercises:**

1. How many sides does a rectangle have?
2. Identify a shape with 6 sides.
3. How many angles does a pentagon have?
4. What do we call an angle less than 90 degrees?
5. Is the shape with 3 sides and 3 angles called a trapezoid?

**Answers:**

1. A rectangle has 4 sides.
2. A shape with 6 sides is a hexagon.
3. A pentagon has 5 angles.
4. An angle less than 90 degrees is called an acute angle.
5. No, the shape with 3 sides and 3 angles is called a triangle.

Remember to check your answers after completing the worksheets and review any mistakes with your teacher or parent. Practice makes perfect, and the more you practice, the better you will become at understanding and solving math problems. Have fun with these exercises!
