**Activity 8: Story Problems**
Solve these simple story problems.

1. If you have 2 apples and your mom gives you 1 more apple, how many apples do you have in total? - Answer: 3 apples
2. You have 4 balloons and 1 pops. How many balloons are left? - Answer: 3 balloons
3. There are 3 ducks in the pond and 2 more ducks join them. How many ducks are in the pond now? - Answer: 5 ducks
4. You are reading a book with 5 pages. You have read 3 pages already. How many pages do you have left to read? - Answer: 2 pages

Let's review what we learned:

- Counting objects and writing numbers helps us understand quantity.
- Recognizing and drawing basic shapes allows us to understand geometry around us.
- Pattern recognition helps us predict what comes next based on the given order.
- Addition and subtraction are the foundations of arithmetic that involve putting together and taking apart numbers.
- Comparing quantities teaches us about more and less.
- Story problems help us apply math to real-life situations.

Keep practicing these math concepts, and you'll be well-prepared for first grade! Great job on completing these activities!
