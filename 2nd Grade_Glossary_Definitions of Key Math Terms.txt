## Definitions of Key Math Terms

Below is a list of key math terms that second-grade students should be familiar with. Each definition is followed by an example exercise with its answer to help students understand and apply the concepts.

### Addition
Addition is the process of finding the total or sum by combining two or more numbers.
**Example:** If you have 3 apples and you get 2 more apples, how many apples do you have now?
**Exercise:** 3 + 2 = ?
**Answer:** 5

### Subtraction
Subtraction is taking one number away from another to find the difference between them.
**Example:** If you have 5 oranges and you give away 2 oranges, how many oranges do you have left?
**Exercise:** 5 - 2 = ?
**Answer:** 3

### Sum
The sum is the result of adding two or more numbers together.
**Example:** What is the sum of 4 and 3?
**Exercise:** 4 + 3 = ?
**Answer:** 7

### Difference
The difference is the result of subtracting one number from another.
**Example:** What is the difference between 8 and 5?
**Exercise:** 8 - 5 = ?
**Answer:** 3

### Equation
An equation is a mathematical sentence showing that two expressions are equal by using an equals sign (=).
**Example:** 2 + 3 = 5 is an equation.
**Exercise:** Write an equation for "three added to four is seven."
**Answer:** 3 + 4 = 7

### Place Value
Place value refers to the value of a digit based on its position in a number. In the number 53, the place value of 5 is tens, and the place value of 3 is ones.
**Example:** In the number 46, what is the place value of 4?
**Exercise:** Write the place value of the digit 6 in the number 67.
**Answer:** Tens

### Even Numbers
Even numbers are whole numbers that can be divided evenly by 2. They end with 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
**Example:** Is 12 an even number?
**Exercise:** Write the next even number after 4.
**Answer:** 6

### Odd Numbers
Odd numbers are whole numbers that cannot be divided evenly by 2. They end with 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
**Example:** Is 7 an odd number?
**Exercise:** Write the next odd number after 9.
**Answer:** 11

### Number Line
A number line is a straight line with numbers placed at equal distances along it, often used for counting or showing the value of numbers.
**Example:** Show the number 7 on a number line.
**Exercise:** Locate the number 2 on a number line and draw an arrow to it.
**Answer:** [Arrow points to the number 2]

### Array
An array is a set of objects or numbers arranged in rows and columns that is used to show multiplication concepts.
**Example:** Draw an array that shows 3 rows of 2 apples.
**Exercise:** How many apples are there in an array with 4 rows and 3 apples in each row?
**Answer:** 12 apples

### Greater Than
The symbol '>' means "greater than." It shows that one number is larger than another.
**Example:** Is 8 greater than 5?
**Exercise:** Fill in the blank: 6 _ 3
**Answer:** 6 > 3

### Less Than
The symbol '<' means "less than." It shows that one number is smaller than another.
**Example:** Is 2 less than 4?
**Exercise:** Fill in the blank: 5 _ 8
**Answer:** 5 < 8

### Equal To
The symbol '=' means "equal to." It shows that two values are the same.
**Example:** Is 4+3 equal to 7?
**Exercise:** Fill in the blank so that the equation is correct: 3 + 4 _ 2 + 5
**Answer:** =

### Measurement
Measurement is the process of finding the length, weight, or volume of an object using units like inches, pounds, or cups.
**Example:** How many inches long is the pencil?
**Exercise:** Measure the length of your math book and write it down in inches.
**Answer:** Answers will vary.

### Data
Data is information that is gathered and used to find out things or answer questions, often organized in charts or graphs.
**Example:** How many students in the class like pizza?
**Exercise:** Make a tally chart to show how many red, blue, and green cars you see.
**Answer:** Answers will vary.
